03

Logo System

Horizontal — on Dark
Horizontal — on Light
Stacked — on Dark
Stacked — on Light
Mark — on Dark
Mark — on Light
Do

Use cream logo on dark backgrounds. Use mud logo on cream/light surfaces. Maintain clear space equal to the xoxo mark height on all sides.

Don't

Don't place logo on busy imagery without an overlay. Don't go below 120px wide (horizontal), 80px (stacked), or 32px (mark).

05

Brand Voice

We write to one person. Not an audience. Not a market segment. The one who is already almost there — who woke up this morning with the same ache they went to sleep with.

The Philosophy

Warm but not soft

We are kind. We are not vague. Warmth without specificity is just noise. The most loving thing we can do is be precise.

Direct but not cold

Short sentences. Clean transitions. No hedging, no apologizing for taking up space. We say the thing.

Honest about ache

We name what it actually feels like. Not what it looks like from the outside. The inside — the specific, embodied, inconvenient truth.

Personal without oversharing

We pull from lived experience. We do not narrate it. The reader should feel the weight, not the burden of our processing.

The Goal

The goal is never conversion.
The goal is recognition.

Conversion is what happens
when someone finally feels recognized.

Not This

"Are you struggling to articulate your unique value proposition in a crowded market?"

"Don't miss this limited opportunity — spots are filling fast!"

"My signature framework will help you unlock your authentic brand story and scale with soul."

This

"You look at your own website and feel nothing. Or worse — a little embarrassed."

"The brand is still out there. Still saying the old thing. And every time someone who isn't quite right slides into your inbox, you feel it."

"This is the work. Eight weeks. One clear process. A brand that finally fits."

Before You Publish

Am I writing to one real person, or to an imaginary audience?

Is this my energy — or am I mimicking what I think this should sound like?

Have I named the felt ache — not just the problem, but how it lives in the body?

Is every word necessary?

Does it read like I'm talking to someone I love, or like I'm performing?

Is the next step clear — and does it feel like relief, not pressure?
